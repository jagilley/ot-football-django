How does Playoff Fantasy Football differ from regular Fantasy Football?

The drafting processes, scoring methods, etc., are all identical to what you'll be familiar with from regular fantasy football. The meta is a bit different, though. While, for instance, it can be useful to draft good players on bad teams in regular fantasy, this strategy may be less useful in playoff fantasy because those bad teams may be more likely to be eliminated quickly (leaving their players unable to score any more points.) Drafting mediocre players on good teams may therefore be much more useful in playoff fantasy than in regular fantasy.

How many people can be in an Overtime Football league?

Because each player must play 3 other players before the Fantasy Super Bowl, each league must have a minimum of 4 players. You'll need to have this quorum before starting the fantasy draft. There is no upper limit on the number of players in a league, but at some point, you'll run out of starting QBs (for instance), and it will become hard to maintain league parity, so we wouldn't recommend having too many more than, say, 8 players in a league.

How does the playoff elimination process work?

We use a condensed version of the regular fantasy tournament method (known in the field of tournament design as a Swiss tournament.) Each player plays 3 other players during the first 3 weeks of the NFL playoffs. At the conclusion of this round, the top two players by record (and secondarily, total points scored) advance to the Fantasy Super Bowl. The winner of the Fantasy Super Bowl wins the league.

What scoring format do you use?

We use half-PPR because we think standard is too biased towards touchdowns, and full PPR is too biased towards (potentially relatively meaningless) receptions. In the playoffs, finding the right balance between these is especially important.

Can I use other scoring formats?

Not right now, but this is feature is on the to-do list.

Can the league creator (aka 'commissioner', 'admin') have a custom team name?

This isn't possible at the moment, for database design reasons. Instead, league creators get a randomly-assigned tongue-in-cheek immutable title.
